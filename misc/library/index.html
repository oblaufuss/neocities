Art Books

Reference

★ Vision and Art: The Biology of Seeing by Margeret Livingstone (2008)
The ultimate guide to color theory and understanding art through a universal biological lens. (Includes large color printed examples--finding a physical copy at your local library strongly reccomended!)
Bridgman's Complete Guide to Drawing from Life by George Bridgman (1952)
A classic of anotomical illustration. (mirror 1) (mirror 2)
Morpho Anatomy for Artists: Fat and Skin Folds by Michel Lauricella (2019)
The art reference book I reccommend to expand Bridgman's lessons to female, fat, old, or very young bodies.

Comics Theory + History

Comics & Sequential Art by Will Eisner (1985)
A series of essays and demonstrations on the language of comic-book techniques. Widely considered the first book on comics theory. (mirror 1) (mirror 2)
★ Understanding Comics: The Invisible Art by Scott McCloud (1993)
An introduction to comics theory in comic form. (Parts of this book emphasize printed page layout--finding a physical copy at your local library strongly reccomended!) (mirror 1)
The Amazing Adventures of Kavalier & Clay by Michael Chabon (2000)
A fictionalized version of the people behind the birth of the American superhero comic genre and historical events which shaped their lives.
Superman Says You Can Slap a Jap: Race and Representation in Comics by Margaret Elizabeth Woodbury (2017)
A Master's thesis on the history of racism and racial progress in mainstream American comics.
