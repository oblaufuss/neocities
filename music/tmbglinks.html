THE INTERNET IS FOR THEY MIGHT BE GIANTS

In building this website, I fell in love with the web 1.0 look and creation ethic, and I've found quite a few sites/pages from TMBG fans of years past. Here's some of the best-preserved. (Favorites with good and/or exclusive content marked with !! and real oddities marked with ??)


    • Live sites:

    • !!- This Might Be a Wiki (The indespensible TMBG resource since 2003.)
    • !!- Museumofidiots (TMBG bootlegs, photos, videos, articles, and extras enough to last a lifetime.)
    • If you maintain your own fansite/page, let me know!

    • Live sites no longer maintained:

    • !!- Biff's TMBG Stuff (Some pics, and MIDI for TMBG, State Songs, and Mono Puff.)
    • TMBG.org (Band info, some strange downloadable games.)
    • !!- Wompedy TMBG (TMBG-themed font downloads, tiled backgrounds, info.)
    • Mono Puff website
    • TDK's Mono Puff Syndicate Shrine
    • Schmitty's TMBG fanpage (click through to get to a page with a bunch of cartoons, mostly fanart)
    • ??- Flansyon-lotor (A conspiracy theory.)
    • ??- The They Might Be Giants Suck Ass Club (This... might be a Buffy the Vampire Slayer fanpage??)
    • alt.music.tmbg messageboard (Searchable back until 1996. I can't link to this without saying that I hope early 2000s user Bibliophilia writes a book someday. I'm enthralled by her outbursts of charmingly graphic biological prose.)

    • Archived sites (2000s):

    • !!- TMBG e-card maker (It won't send it, sadly, but it will still make one.)

    • Pisces' TMBG rants

    • ??- The TMBG Conspirator ("The Onion"-like parody articles. Personal favorites: "John Flansburgh Declared Dead By Idiot" and "A Paid Message From the Feed The John Linnell Foundation".)
