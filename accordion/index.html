I Have Played Accordion For:

Why do I play accordion? Because accordions are awesome, of course. And several of my favorite musical artists do, too. But it was this video of the New England Accordion Museum that captured me fully. I hope to one day visit the museum in person to thank that man and to see some of his collection. (Also to see the Hohnerette that he has, because gee whiz.)

If I've convinced you to play accordion (or at least convinced you that they're super cool!), then I consider my work here done. Check out some of these neat links.

    • The Palmer-Hughes books, designed to bring the beginner musician through the process of learning accordion from scratch. These are what I'm using! I ripped 'em from somewhere and now I'm hosting the first 4 on my site for lucky you. (Book 1) (Book 2) (Book 3) (Book 4)
    • If those are too easy then try for some fancy fingerwork. Here's a PDF of Hanon accordion exercises.
    • Hans Palm's Accordion Page. This is one of my favorite accordion sites out there (even if it's only "out there" via wayback machine now.) Useful info, interesting information, and a page of accordion jokes!
    • Accordion Links dot com. Exactly what it says on the tin.
    • The Accordion subreddit. Has helped me out with simple questions and advice many a time.
    • Accordion Revival. Everything I know about the guts of accordions and how to take care of them is stuff I've read from this incredibly detailed, expert site. This guy knows what he's talking about.
